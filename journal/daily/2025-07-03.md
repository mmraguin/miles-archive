# Daily Journal — Thursday, 2025-07-03

## Journal
I fainted on Tuesday night after ecstatic dance. It was the first time I got rushed to the ER after fainting.

I know that for a lot of people, losing consciousness isn’t a big deal because you shut down and recover right after, but I don’t know why it’s taking me so long to recover. I’ve been going over what happened right before it over and over again.

It’s also not the first time that it’s happened to me… Maybe it’s exactly because of that? The fact that I’ve been through it and, on all those occasions, there was something bad that happened before. Twice was because I oversmoked. Another was an injection. Another one was because I hit my back and head.

At least this time I was more aware of it coming and I hugged mom right before. I guess subconsciously I knew what was happening or what was going to… I guess I hugged her because I was scared?

I remember when I first felt it coming. We were outside South Bank in Westgate and right before, I was talking about Mik. Then Lee asked me to reach my hand out to feel the energy she was playing around with coming from ecstatic dance class.

I felt it warm on my hand, and there are no other words I can use to describe what I felt other than how it could’ve felt like the energy went straight into my body. I felt it in my chest. And that’s where the line gets blurry, because it was also the same initial feeling I get when anxious or panicky.

I really thought it was just me feeling the energy, but maybe it was anxiety already? I’d like to think it wasn’t caused by the whole “energy ball” thing, because how can something like that cause my body and brain to just fully shut down?

As soon as I felt it, we were going back inside and my thoughts already started racing. I remember mom saying she wanted another drink and me saying I wanted to go home if it was okay. Then I said I needed to sit down in one of the tables outside and I immediately put my head on the table.

I don’t remember what I was saying, but the next thing I knew was that I thought I was just shaking or shivering, something that happens when I feel like I’m going through a panic attack. But my arm was shaking uncontrollably, like a tic or like I was anxiously shaking my arm off.

I remember Lee asking me if I was doing it on purpose, and I was consciously telling her I wasn’t. But my hands tend to quiver when I get an attack. I knew I wanted to go to the car and lie down, so I told mom.

I remember leaning on her while walking and saying after a few steps that it was coming again. Now that I look back at it, I don’t know why we hugged—wouldn’t it have taken more time? Then the next thing I knew, I was hearing her calling my name but I didn’t want to wake up because I knew I’d wake up in another dream.

It didn’t feel like reality. It almost felt like a nightmare, the way I could hear my mom’s voice and hear my name… I didn’t want to wake up.

Next thing is I realized I passed out, remembered where we were and hearing Bee saying even my feet were white. There were people behind us I think? I put my weight on mom as we walked to the car and I was feeling really cold from the essential oils that she put on my back.

I was thirsty and still very confused, disoriented, and weak. I remember my mom saying that she wanted to go to the ER, and me telling her I was fine and that I just wanted to go home and sleep it off. She insisted, saying that Bee’s sister has seizures, so if it was something that alarmed Bee, we might as well be sure.

We got to the ER and mom wanted to park, so I asked if she could stay with me. I was wheelchaired in. They got my blood pressure and sent me to a bed right away.

I asked for water but the nurse said they weren’t sure if I could drink yet, depending on tests they’d have to run. I was still disoriented, cold, weak, and afraid. Everything about it was a big fear. I’d come to terms with even dying already. There was no way I could panic because I was just too exhausted.

I also reasoned with myself that if anything were to happen, I was in the best place for that. I just really wanted to go home. They connected all these wires to me and tried to get my blood pressure again and kept on failing. They said they couldn’t find it. They tried several times and resorted to doing it manually.

The doctor finally came in and asked me all these questions and ordered blood tests and an MRI. The last time I had an MRI, I hated every bit of it. It was loud, it was long, it felt claustrophobic and it was just terrible.

I had hope that maybe, in the last 15 years, it would’ve gotten better, but the only improvement was the fact that I had a little bit of music. I don’t think that I’ll ever hear Larry the same way anymore. It was faster and longer than I’d expected, all at the same time.

Mom asked me if I wanted her to message Mik and for some reason, I didn’t want her to. I didn’t want to deal with his questions that I’d be too weak to answer. I didn’t want him to come (if ever he was even going to) and deal with his energy.

And then my mind went on to wondering what it would’ve been like if I was still with Ting. He’d have gone in a heartbeat. He’d have asked me what I needed and would’ve told me he loved me. I knew I wasn’t going to get any of that with Mik, but just a bunch of questions instead.

I didn’t want to problem solve, I just wanted someone to care. Everyone who was there cared and I didn’t want anyone to feel like they needed to take care of him.

After my MRI, I felt like I had to update him, and sure enough, the flurry of questions arrived. I’m not saying that he doesn’t care, I’m just saying that he was so focused on figuring out what caused it and what to do next that it sounded so void of presence and acknowledgement of what was going on in that specific moment. Not once did he acknowledge it was a scary moment, or that it was emotionally and physically taxing.

The next day, he did check up on me—asking the usual: how I was feeling. Then more questions about what happened. I understand that it’s not easy for someone to be comforting through text, but he also could’ve called or even visited me.

I told him I was going to treat myself to ice cream and he just said it was a good idea. Like, am I talking to a friend? He never once offered to visit me, send me anything, or anything—just resolved to texting.

I mean, I get it, it’s just not him. He doesn’t think that way and maybe I’m the one who’s expecting someone to be a person they’re not. But it would just feel nice to have someone like that. Bare minimum.

When he was sick, I sent him coconut water and Pocari Sweat, even if his mom was there to take care of him. Different people, I guess.

I haven’t seen him in weeks, and not once did he say he loved me, missed me, or wanted to see me. He’d argue that I wouldn’t either, but it’s always me first. And I’m tired of just being reciprocated or reciprocating.

I haven’t spoken to Alex Lietz and when he found out, he immediately said he could swing by the house. I just have too many friends who (and I know I shouldn’t) I can compare him to, and it just isn’t it.

I’ve just been festering in all of these negative thoughts and feelings about him and the relationship. I’m having a really difficult time seeing a silver lining other than “he’s rich.” Is that the life I want?
