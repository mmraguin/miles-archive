# Daily Journal — Friday, 2024-04-12

## Journal
I knew it waking up today—it was going to be one of those days. True enough, the insecurities crept in and almost became a fixation. Not thin enough. Not healthy enough. Not pretty enough. Not enough friends. It spirals down to not feeling worth it at all.

I don’t even know if it’s all in my head, if I’m blowing things out of proportion, or if these are real self-evaluations based on what I observe. I know that comparing myself to strangers on social media is toxic for anyone’s mental health. But what if they’re not strangers? What if they’re people who were once in my life, or people who are now in the lives of people who used to matter to me?

I’ve been melancholic and nostalgic about how my life has shifted over the last 10 years. I’m not mourning my old life, but I miss having it. Being careless. Feeling like I had direction. Feeling like the world was there for the taking. It wasn’t about being invincible—it was the freedom and opportunity, the sense of staying in my lane while figuring it out.

Why was I less affected back then, when now I feel more accepting? Why does it feel harder to bounce back? Like I’m stuck. Limited. And I regret taking it for granted. I was young, and I thought that life was going to be mine forever. That’s the human flaw, right? Narrow tunnel vision while being lost in everything else.

Even when I can rationalize it, philosophize my own condition, I still can’t shift how I feel about myself or where I’m at. There are so many variables. Even the things I can control, I can’t do alone. Or maybe I just want too much of a drastic change, influenced by how others live or how their lives look.

I know all this, but it doesn’t make me want it less. It’s that human disease: “when I get [fill in], then I will be [fill in].” We pass time telling ourselves we’re planning, setting things in motion, but are we really? Or are we waiting for the “right time”? For a miracle? Or are we telling ourselves we should be content because everything has its reason?

How much of it is our will, our strength?

There’s so much I want to accomplish, see, and experience. But at the same time, there’s a growing part of me that just wants a simple life. To be content. To stop running after something I think I want—or should want—but that doesn’t really align with my values anymore.

I’ve been in Subic for about 4 days now, doing yoga every day since I got here. It’s been nice to spend time with mom and to feel at home and comfortable.
