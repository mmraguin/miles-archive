# Daily Journal — Friday, 2024-07-12

## Journal
It’s officially not my birthday anymore and it feels really weird. I’m really grateful that the salubong was pretty much how I wanted it to be—chill, no pressure, just family. The people who greeted me were no more, no less than those I expected, which doesn’t upset me or even surprise me.

I think it’s the fact that I wanted something so unceremonious that I’m afraid I’ll forget this birthday years from now. But then again, it really is what I wanted, which feels the most weird to me because I always wanted to celebrate my birthday.

What really meant a lot to me was the fact that some people’s greetings were a lot more than a “happy birthday,” and even though it was honestly just 3 people, what they said really made me feel seen.

It has been a fucking rough 2024 so far, and I feel like I’m both trying to live despite it all, but also that trying to live feels like I’m only living at 20%. But on the other hand, it’s also not as if I’d live the 80% to the full every day. Like, what would I be doing if I didn’t have panic attacks? Or what would I be doing if I didn’t fear getting my spots again?

Why do I get so anxious about my spots? It always feels like such a failure every time I get them—like 2 steps forward, 3 steps back. And then the panic attacks that just feel so overwhelming, like time couldn’t get any slower in the moment.

How do I slow my mind and my thoughts and fears down? How do I appease myself before it escalates… without freaking out that it’s about to escalate? How do I just feel healthy and normal again?

I don’t feel like myself. Or at most, I feel like a shadow of myself. And maybe that’s why I don’t really feel like this birthday is something worth celebrating. On top of the fact that I don’t feel like there are people beyond immediate family to truly celebrate with.

Again, it all really boils down to there not being much to celebrate. If anything, this birthday feels like a reminder that I am either living a life not worth living, living a life that doesn’t feel like mine (in the sense that this isn’t what I wanted), or living a life that constantly reminds me I’m wasting away and there’s nothing notable, memorable, or life-changing.

Maybe those realizations bring a sense of desperation to get over a hump that feels like it will never end. Or maybe I just need to accept that this is my reality now and go from there instead of trying to get the past back.

It’s just hard to stomach that on my birthday, my actual, most honest thoughts are about the worthiness of even being still alive. And I’m totally aware that’s dark, morbid, self-fulfilling, and pathetic. But that’s why I haven’t been able to write anything down in the last few months.
