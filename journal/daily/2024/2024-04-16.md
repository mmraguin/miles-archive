# Daily Journal — Tuesday, 2024-04-16

## Journal
A little more than a week in Subic and I’m starting to get bored. Which frustrates me, because I’m supposed to enjoy this, right? This is what I wanted all along. Plus, I don’t know when I’ll get this much time off again without the pressure of figuring out my financials right away.

Still, the looming anxiety about going back to Tribal is there—and I know I don’t want to. At least, I know I don’t want to work under Chichi anymore. I miss some people, but not enough to want to go back. Or maybe it’s not even about Tribal. Maybe I just don’t want an office job anymore. I like doing things on my own time. And there are plenty of people who earn outside corporate, right?

I probably need to keep beefing up my freelance work and consider living off that. It gives me creative freedom while letting me control my time and output. I need to figure out an exit strategy, but maybe keep one foot in the door—like freelancing under them for extra income. I don’t care for the career path anymore, especially if it’s just in the Philippines. At the end of the day, it’s the same everywhere, and if it’s just about “learning,” I know it’s not worth it.

If it’s about starting something, I’d rather freelance and outsource creatives than build an agency. Some business ideas stick with me—like the coconut dessert stall, the scarves, or even perfume. My concern is whether I can run them independently from Mik, because you never know what might happen.

I also want to figure out passive income streams from what I already have: Nonon planners, digital products, selling them individually or in bundles. Maybe when my crypto grows, I can cash out and reinvest into something else that generates more. But all of those ideas feel small, intangible. Maybe I’m just not business-minded. Maybe I’m just good at ideas and need someone to run the operations. Or maybe I lean into the housewife role. Be more centered on home and family, which is something I’ve kind of wanted anyway.

People have told me I feel lighter since leaving work—that’s a good sign. But what holds me back most is my health. The legs, the vasculitis. I don’t know when it’ll flare, and when it does, the anxiety is heavy. It stops me from fully enjoying life. Without alcohol as a crutch, stress feels sharper. I can’t avoid stress, so I need better coping. Maybe medicine for a while, just to reset my baseline.

I want to know what causes it and how to avoid it. Stress makes it worse. Alcohol aggravates it. But what does that have to do with blood and my immune system? Why do such small triggers have such big effects? In India, alcohol was involved, standing was involved, but no obvious stress other than fearing the spots.

I wish there was a cure so I could live normally again. Maybe this is my body and brain purging from the pandemic years—when I powered through without thinking about the future. Or maybe it’s the vaccine, which makes sense if it’s an immune overreaction. But that’s defeating, because it’s already in my system. Do vaccines eventually leave the body? As kids we had boosters. People still take flu shots. Maybe this is all part of something bigger.

Maybe it’s God’s will. A wake-up call. To take care of my body. To value my life. To align myself with something bigger than Tribal. To embrace change before it’s too late.

What I know is I don’t want to spend the rest of my life working for companies that aren’t mine. I don’t want to be a cog in the machine. I want to build my own machine. One I don’t dread waking up for.
