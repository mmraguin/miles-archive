## Journal
What brought me to yoga? And how?

I don't even really know. Maybe it's proving to myself that I can commit to goals that I set out for myself, more than the desire or need to workout or move per se. I guess it's really that more than anything.

Today, I wish it were about something more intentional or purposeful, but it wasn't. I want to show myself that I can stick to things that don't always feel good while I'm doing them. Hopefully I can get back to the point that it's more about needing it and that it's part of my needed things and routines, more than just trying to stick to it.

Of course, it's also partly because I still want to lose weight and just be more active generally. I don't want my body to feel old, rusty, or limited by my current lifestyle, or worse, my physical conditions right now.

I guess it's more about teaching my mind and my body to adjust and adapt to the big changes in how my life is. I want to be done feeling like a victim of my autoimmune condition and whatever has been causing me to faint. And I don't want to always be living in fear and avoidance for self preservation.

Maybe this is my way of feeling in control again, in some way or another. To feel like I can do the small things in life first on my own terms, and move things around without being a hindrance to anyone else.

Then maybe, just maybe, I can do life again.
