## Narrative
Fever broke — that's the headline for today. Still congested, still coughing occasionally, but the phlegm is clearing and the worst of the illness feels behind me. Spent the day mostly sedentary, working at my computer on SM-related tasks — a lot of Claude, Excel, and PowerPoint — while also doing work on the journal app. Despite sitting all day, the bevel data tells a cleaner story than I felt: the stress scores are high because my body is still running an immune response in the background, not because I consciously felt stressed. Both things are true at the same time.

Ended the day on a three-and-a-half-hour call with Dre and Casey. It was supposed to be work but lasted about three minutes on that before turning into office gossip and Balisin trip talk. Everyone seems genuinely excited — a lot of them are already packed, which I find a bit excessive but understandable. I listened in and out while still working, so I wasn't fully drained by it.

The trip is sitting heavy on me though. I found out I'm rooming with Chi Chi and my boss, and that we're flying out of Clark on Saturday, which means an earlier wake-up than I'd planned. The logistics are stressful, but the deeper thing is something else. I'm dreading having to perform for four days straight — being "on" socially with people who aren't my closest friends, in a setting where there's no real off switch. None of my office mates have autoimmune conditions, and because my illness isn't visible, there's an assumption that I'm the same person I was before — someone who stayed up late and drank and was one of the last ones standing. I'm not that person anymore and I don't want to pretend to be.

There's also a quieter thing underneath all of it. On previous company trips, my ex was there — he used to work at the same company — and he was my anchor in those situations. My person in the room. That relationship ended almost four years ago and I'm not holding onto it, but the feeling of having someone in your corner in a crowd is something I haven't replaced. Walking into the same kind of trip without that is noticeable, even if I can't fully articulate it.

I know I'm probably blowing this out of proportion. Anxiety fills in the blanks with worst-case scenarios, and I'm projecting four days of exhaustion onto a trip that hasn't happened yet, from a body that's still recovering. It'll likely be more chill than I'm imagining. But the performing worry is real — that's the thing that will drain me fastest, not the socializing itself or even the bad sleep, but the effort of pretending I have capacity I don't have.

Friday is going to be its own challenge — in the office from 1pm through 7 or 8pm for a town hall, then leaving for Clark the next morning. Trying not to arrive at Saturday already empty.

Tonight: finished work when Claude's usage limit kicked me out at 10pm, which was actually a useful forcing function. Finishing dinner, shower, bed. Wind-down is intentional and the plan is right.

Couldn't sleep and ended up writing at 2am. Had a long conversation with Mommy 2 — the kind that wanders and lands somewhere good. We talked about vanity and getting older, about being careful with money and always pulling out your principal once you've earned from it. About making decisions with your head first, then your heart. About letting anger go and keeping your center of peace. She told me her love story with Gpops — how sometimes love follows, how not everything starts or ends as a fairytale. I don't fully know how to practice that part because I still want a nice love story. But it's worth holding. Not everything has to begin the way you imagined it.
