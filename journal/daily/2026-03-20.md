I'm pretty sure it broke by evening; I drank ginger tea and added lemon to my water, which helped. No work today. Energy was low across the board, which felt appropriate given everything.

Ate lightly — a banana around noon and chicken with rice at around 3pm. Simple, but I got something in. Meds are on track; I ordered a refill so I won't be running short.

Mild flare on the foot yesterday, nothing dramatic. Joints otherwise okay.

The thing sitting heaviest tonight is next weekend. There's a company trip — two nights on Balisin Island — and I'm dreading it. I've already bowed out of other international trips for the same reasons: agoraphobia tied to my syncope history, not feeling safe being far from an exit, not wanting to get dizzy or pass out in front of colleagues with no one around who knows what to do. My boss is pushing me to go this time, which is its own kind of pressure — and she knows about the syncope, which makes the pressure harder to sit with.

Part of me wants to go. Not to prove anything to anyone at the office, but to prove something to myself — that the agoraphobia isn't running the show. I know the beach setting should be relatively chill. I'm at peace with sitting out activities, saying I don't feel well if I need to, and not performing wellness for anyone. The goal is just to show up and not have a panic attack.

Practical plan coming together: four liters of Pocari Sweat for electrolytes, full meds packed with buffer, Xanor on hand for anxiety. Worth remembering that Xanor on a hot beach with active vasculitis and a syncope history carries some blood pressure and sedation risk — take it mindfully. The bigger gap is not having a safe person on the trip who knows what to do in an emergency, and no clear exit plan if things go wrong. Those are worth sorting out before I go.

The framing that felt right: this isn't white-knuckling through something to prove a point. It's showing up, pacing, and advocating for myself if needed. That's enough.

Winding down late. Tomorrow is Methotrexate day.
