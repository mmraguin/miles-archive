# Daily Journal — Wednesday, 2024-06-19

## Journal
Therapy today was something I was really looking forward to because it felt like an act of desperation, a last-ditch effort to figure out what’s causing my panic attacks and what to do about them. I think I must’ve overprepared, and it turned out for the better because I got so much out of it. Buttering her up towards the end also helped her be a bit more invested in me.

The biggest takeaways were that my body is storing a lot of negative emotions and I need to find ways to release them while also facing the emotions my mind keeps shortcutting. I also learned that beneath situational triggers, there might be underlying emotions that aren’t obvious. Something could trigger me even if it looks unrelated at first glance, because my body or brain still links them enough to respond.

Looking back, a lot of my attacks happen when I’m with Mik. Partly because I’m with him a lot, but maybe also because he triggers something deeper underneath. During an unplanned debrief with Mica right after, I realized there are so many unmet emotional needs I brush off daily. I know he struggles with things like words of affirmation or physical touch, so I don’t want to pressure him. But things like saying “I love you,” “thank you,” or complimenting me are very important to me.

It’s not the first time I’ve journaled about feeling taken for granted or unappreciated. Even when we talk about it, and even if it makes sense logically, it doesn’t feel reassuring in my body. I don’t feel soothed, safe, or secure in my place in the relationship.

I brought up the pressure of being good enough to meet his family from India, and he just said the pressure is something I made up. That if I feel otherwise, it’s my fault. Of course I think about him wanting me there, but it feels more like I have to be there than him truly wanting me there.

Another source of insecurity is his ex. Small things built up: his mom calling me by her name, his cousins clearly loving her and still being in contact. It makes me want to prove I’m better. It ties back to how I don’t always feel like he’s fully invested in me. His only response was, “why would I be here otherwise?” Instead of reassurance, I’m met with logic. My feelings always get filtered through how they make him feel or how my reasoning is flawed, not with validation.

He gave me closet space, sure. He talks about “our” life—though now it’s shifting back to “my”—but it all triggers the same lack of reassurance.

Another anxiety trigger is being around his friends. I feel pressured to do drugs with them. For me, drugs were always about escape, and I was raised to think they were bad. Doing them feels unsafe because of both conditioning and actual bad experiences. Passing out, stitches, freaking my friends out, feeling like I was going to die.

Even when we tried E together—something I’m glad I crossed off my list but wouldn’t repeat—I didn’t enjoy it. I almost passed out, and instead of comforting me, he talked about how he and his ex did it to get closer. That made me feel even more inadequate.

I don’t know which of these are personal issues I need to work through and which are true incompatibilities. He doesn’t force me to do anything, but the social pressure is there. And writing about it now, I can feel my muscles tense and my heartbeat speed up. It’s the feeling of not being acknowledged—of having my comfort zones dismissed under logic.

Am I being manipulated or gaslit? He often says the “right” thing, but it doesn’t align with how I feel. Maybe my definition of intimacy and shared experience (like food) clashes with his (like drugs). He doesn’t even do them often, but the fact that E is treated like a special event and C as casual just adds pressure.

It conflicts with my core beliefs and also triggers me because I know how easily I can spiral back into abusing benzos and alcohol. Maybe I just need to be firmer with my boundaries and sense of self. If I had a stronger grip on my identity in those moments, I wouldn’t feel the need to oblige for acceptance. Maybe this ties back to the ex again, too.

There’s a lot of work I need to do. But some of these also feel like deep incompatibilities. If I were with someone else, would I even be facing these?

Still, maybe this is the turning point. Learning to identify and assert my boundaries. Taking space. Trusting my sense of self. That’s the most respectable thing I can do—for myself and as a person.
